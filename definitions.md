# Old Words
> In alpha-order
* Adulation
  * Fake flattery, Noun(sycophantic)
* Atavistic
  * ~throwback
  * Displaying characteristics of a previous cultural era/form
* Behest.
  * Def: command or directive. Earnest or strongly worded request.
  * Usage: At his/her behest I...
* Catatonic
  * Relating to catatonia (which is a state of abnormality of movement related
    to a disturbed mental state
  * An immobile/unresponsive stupor.
* Citadel
  * Strong point, fortress.
* Conduit
  * A channel or pipe for carrying water or other liquid.
  * > I am happy to be learning this work in a "non-technical" way after
    hearing it on Grey's :)
* Deft
  * Neatly skillful
  * Demonstrating skill and cleverness.
* Disquieting
  * Inducing feelings of anxiousness or worry.
  * To make uneasy
* Fatuous
  * Silly and pointless.
* Fungible.
  * Easily substitutable. Interchangeable.
  * E.g., money is usually fungible. Dollars earmarked for gas can easily be
    turned into grocery money.
* Illustrious.
  * Well-known, respected, admired for past achievements.
  * Notably brilliant (also for achievements).
* Imperious.
  * Def: Assuming power without proper justification or authority.
    Overbearing.
* Inanition
  * Exhaustion caused from lack of nourishment.
  * Lacking spirit, vigor, or enthusiasm.
* Indelible
  * ~of pens~ making marks that are not able to be removed.
  * Other terms, not easily forgotten/removable.
* Jettison
  * to throw away something or get rid of it (orig it seemed to deal with ship
    and airplane cargo in times of distress)
* Lithe
  * Thin graceful agile
* Mellifluous
  * Def: (of a voice our sound) pleasant to hear. sweet or musical.
  * Usage: Their mellifluous voice/writing...
  * Seems to be the opposite of cacophonous.
* Mendacious.
  * Def: Not truthful. Lying
  * As a Noun: Mendacity
* Penchant
  * Def: A strong or habitual liking for something
  * Usage: He/she had a penchant for doing X
* Periphrastic
  _of writing styles_ indirect and circuitous.
* Placid
  * Mild, undisturbed, calm, peaceful
* Providential
  1. Occurring at a favorable time
    * The providential storm canceled school!
  1. Involving divine intervention(
    * God's providential care...
* Pseudo-
  * Def: Fake. Not real.
* Quasi-
  * Def: partially, but not complete. Almost
  * Differs from pseudo- in that quasi- is something is close to something else
    but is not (possibly, yet) there. quasi does not mean fake.
* Reticent
  * To be silent.
  * Apparently second have of the word `ticent` is related to tacet.
* Saccharine
  * Def: Overly sweet. Almost artificially. Used to note something that seems
    to be superflously nice/sweet.
* Scrabble
  * to scratch or grope around with one's fingers to collect/find something.
  * ~rummage ~scavenge
* Self-flagellation
  * Excessive criticism of oneself.
* Spastic
  * Related to spasms. Shaky. Uncontrolled.
  * Is offensive in Britain (but not in the US?).
* Superfluous
  * Def: Over the top. Unnecessarily overfilled.
* Temerity.
  * The willingness to do or say something to upset/shock people.
* Zeitgeist
  * Spirit of a period time (characterization)
* Zenith
  * At the time when it's highest or most powerful.
  * ~apex
