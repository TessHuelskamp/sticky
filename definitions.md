# Words
* Fungible.
  * Easily substitutable. Interchangeable.
  * E.g., money is usually fungible. Dollars earmarked for gas can easily be
    turned into grocery money.
* Imperious.
  * Def: Assuming power without proper justification or authority.
    Overbearing.
* Mendacious.
  * Def: Not truthful. Lying
  * As a Noun: Mendacity
* Temerity.
  * The willingness to do or say something to upset/shock people.
* Behest.
  * Def: command or directive. Earnest or strongly worded request.
  * Usage: At his/her behest I...
* Penchant
  * Def: A strong or habitual liking for something
  * Usage: He/she had a penchant for doing X
* mellifluous
  * Def: (of a voice our sound) pleasant to hear. sweet or musical.
  * Usage: Their mellifluous voice/writing...
  * Seems to be the opposite of cacophonous.
* Saccharine
  * Def: Overly sweet. Almost artificially. Used to note something that seems
    to be superflously nice/sweet.
* Superfluous
  * Def: Over the top. Unnecessarily overfilled.
* quasi-
  * Def: partially, but not complete. Almost
  * Differs from pseudo in that quasi is something is close to something else
    but is not (possibly, yet) there. quasi does not mean fake.
* Pseudo-
  * Def: Fake. Not real.
* Jettison
  * to throw away something or get rid of it (orig it seemed to deal with ship
    and airplane cargo in times of distress)
